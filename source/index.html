About

About Product Design

The job of a Product Designer is to make things that people want. We investigate user problems, invent and test potential solutions, and ultimately design and ship products or features.

I am a generalist with competency in multiple disciplines, including user research, testing, interaction design, visual design, prototyping and software development.

Professional Focus

I specialize in designing new product concepts. I work with founders, management and customers to develop a product's value proposition, go-to-market strategy and critical features. Then I design, code and iterate until the users and stakeholders are happy.

I have worked as both a team lead and individual contributor on eight early-stage product development efforts. Many of these products had no predicate and attempted to define new product categories.

Philosophy

My philosophy is rooted in IDEO's human-centered design process and Y Combinator's "do things that don't scale" approach to startups.

I've built products that failed because they solved the wrong problem. Never again. My work is anchored in ideation, prototyping and user testing. I am committed to shipping products and features that create measurable value for the customers and business.

Skills & Tools

Twelve years design experience spanning identity, visual design and UI/UX. I draw in Illustrator, design interfaces in Sketch, wireframe with Invision, and prototype interactions with Framer. I relish any opportuntiy to work on logos and iconography though it's a small part of my work.

I am a compentant developer with experience in iOS (Objective-C), Ruby on Rails and JavaScript.

Background

I've worked on hardware, educational products, web projects, and most recently, consumer software and mobile apps. Though the tools may change, I've found that a human-centered design process results in high quality products, no matter the medium or industry.

I don't have a traditional work history. For the last three years I've run my own micro-agency that provides marketing and product design services to clients while allowing me to pursue side projects and entrepreneurial efforts.

Areas of Interest

I can get excited about any project if it tackles an interesting problem in a clever way, especially if the solution involves innovation in interaction and interface. I closely follow the fields of education, podcasting, social games, climate-change adaption and food systems.

For Fun

There's nothing better than hitting the road in my homemade camper van. You'll often find me in the sauana and cold plunge at Archimedes Banya. And I spend at least one evening per week watching X-Files reruns and working on a line of mass-produced cardboard fashion accessories.

Education

I have a BA in Environmental Economics from UCSC (2009), where I achieved high honors for undergraduate thesis work on municipal solar projects. It took me six years to graduate because I worked full time through college at a product development consultancy. That’s where I learned how to design, manage teams and ship products.

I've also attended software development training to increase my technical capabilities. Over the last three years I have completed more than 6-months of development training with Dev Bootcamp (Ruby on Rails) and Mobile Makers Academy (iOS).
